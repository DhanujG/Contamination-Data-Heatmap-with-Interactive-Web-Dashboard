1-Butanol is also known as butyl alcohol and is a flammable liquid that is produced in large amounts by and for manufacturing companies. 1-butanol is commonly added to plastics, hydraulic fluids, and detergent formulations as well as extractants for drug companies and is used as an additive in medicines.{1} This contaminant can cause headaches if inhaled for a short period of time, as well as skin, eye, nose, and throat irritation. Repeated exposure can also lead to adverse eye effects and hearing loss. 

1. EPA Chemicals in the Environment, “1-Butanol”, US Environmental Protection Agency
